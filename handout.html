Research Data Security 101

John D. Martin III

2024-11-17

This presentation covers the critical aspects of securing research data, emphasizing the protection of sensitive information such as Personally Identifiable Information (PII) and Protected Health Information (PHI). It highlights the need for aligning research data security practices with industry standards, treating research data as a vital institutional asset, and ensuring compliance with Federal guidelines.

Key Questions for Consideration

  • Is our research data treated as a strategic institutional asset?
  • Do our current data security practices align with industry standards like NIST?
  • How are we minimizing the risks associated with handling PII and PHI?

Definitions

Personally Identifiable Information (PII)

PII includes any data that can identify an individual, such as names, addresses, and social security numbers. Its protection is governed by regulations like the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA).

Protected Health Information (PHI)

PHI, as defined by the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA), includes medical records, health insurance information, and other health-related data linked to an individualâ€™s identity. HIPAA mandates strict handling and consent requirements for PHI.

Data Governance

Data governance refers to the framework of policies and procedures that dictate how data is managed, shared, and protected within an organization. Effective data governance ensures compliance, data quality, and security.

NIST Standards

The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) provides guidelines for information security through publications like NIST SP 800-53. These guidelines help institutions implement robust security controls and align with Federal requirements.
